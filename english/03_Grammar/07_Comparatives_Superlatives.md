# Comparatives and Superlatives

## Rule

Use comparatives to compare two things. Use superlatives to compare one thing with a group.

## Formulas

| Type | Short adjective | Long adjective |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| Comparative | adj + **-er** | **more** + adj |
| Superlative | adj + **-est** | **the most** + adj |

## Examples

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| cheap | cheaper | the cheapest |
| expensive | more expensive | the most expensive |
| good | better | the best |
| bad | worse | the worst |

## Common Mistakes at Band 3.0

1. ❌ She is **more taller** than me. → ✅ She is **taller** than me.
2. ❌ This is **the most beautifulest**. → ✅ This is **the most beautiful**.
3. ❌ He is **more intelligent than** her is. → ✅ He is **more intelligent than** her.
4. ❌ Hanoi is **more big** than Hue. → ✅ Hanoi is **bigger** than Hue.

## Practice Sentences

1. A car is __________ (expensive) than a bike. (more expensive)
2. This is __________ (good) movie I have ever seen. (the best)
3. She runs __________ (fast) than her brother. (faster)
4. Today is __________ (hot) day of the year. (the hottest)
5. My house is __________ (small) than yours. (smaller)

## IELTS Context

- **Writing Task 1:** "The price of oil was **higher** in 2020 **than** in 2010."
- **Writing Task 2:** "Online learning is **more convenient** than traditional classes."
