# Conditionals

## Rule

Conditionals show possible or imaginary situations and their results.

## Formulas

| Type | If clause | Main clause | Use |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Zero | If + present simple | present simple | general truth | If you heat water, it boils. |
| First | If + present simple | will + V | real future | If it rains, I will stay home. |
| Second | If + past simple | would + V | imaginary present | If I were rich, I would travel. |

## Common Mistakes at Band 3.0

1. ❌ If I **will see** him, I **will tell** him. → ✅ If I **see** him, I **will tell** him.
2. ❌ If she **study** hard, she **will pass**. → ✅ If she **studies** hard, she **will pass**.
3. ❌ If I **am** you, I **will** accept. → ✅ If I **were** you, I **would** accept.

## Practice Sentences

1. If I __________ (have) time, I __________ (help) you. (first conditional)
2. If water __________ (reach) 100°C, it __________ (boil). (zero conditional)
3. If I __________ (be) taller, I __________ (play) basketball. (second conditional)
4. She __________ (call) you if she __________ (need) help. (first conditional)
5. If they __________ (not / hurry), they __________ (miss) the bus. (first conditional)

## IELTS Context

- **Writing Task 2:** "If the government **invests** more in education, society **will** benefit."
- **Speaking Part 3:** "If I **were** the minister, I **would**..."
