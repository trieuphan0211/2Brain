# Linkers and Signposting

## Rule

Linkers connect ideas and show the relationship between sentences.

## Common Linkers

| Function | Linkers |
|----------|---------|
| Addition | and, also, in addition, furthermore, moreover |
| Contrast | but, however, although, on the other hand |
| Reason | because, since, as, due to |
| Result | so, therefore, as a result, consequently |
| Example | for example, for instance, such as |
| Sequence | first, second, finally, in conclusion |

## Common Mistakes at Band 3.0

1. ❌ I like him **although** he is rude. (awkward) → ✅ **However**, I don't like his behavior.
2. ❌ **Because** I was tired. → ✅ I went to bed early **because** I was tired.
3. ❌ I like coffee **and also** tea. → ✅ I like coffee. **In addition**, I like tea.
4. ❌ **But** the weather was bad. We stayed home. → ✅ **However**, the weather was bad, **so** we stayed home.

## Practice Sentences

1. I like studying English. __________, I find it difficult. (However)
2. She stayed at home __________ she was sick. (because)
3. __________, I will discuss the advantages. (First)
4. The city is crowded. __________, it is expensive. (Moreover)
5. He studied hard. __________, he passed the exam. (As a result)

## IELTS Context

- **Writing Task 2:** Use at least 3–4 different linkers in each essay.
- **Speaking:** Use simple linkers like "because," "so," "but," "however."
