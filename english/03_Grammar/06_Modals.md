# Modals

## Rule

Modals express ability, possibility, permission, advice, and obligation.

## Formulas

| Modal | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| can | ability | I **can** swim. |
| could | past ability, polite request | I **could** run faster when I was young. |
| may | possibility, permission | It **may** rain. |
| might | weaker possibility | It **might** be true. |
| should | advice | You **should** study more. |
| must | strong obligation | You **must** wear a seatbelt. |

## Common Mistakes at Band 3.0

1. ❌ I **can** to swim. → ✅ I **can** swim.
2. ❌ She **must** to go. → ✅ She **must** go.
3. ❌ I **can** swimming. → ✅ I **can** swim.
4. ❌ You **should** to rest. → ✅ You **should** rest.

## Practice Sentences

1. I __________ (can / speak) French. (can)
2. You __________ (should / see) a doctor. (should)
3. It __________ (might / rain) later. (might)
4. We __________ (must / finish) by 5 p.m. (must)
5. __________ I __________ (could / use) your phone? (could)

## IELTS Context

- **Writing Task 2:** "The government **should** invest more in public transport."
- **Speaking Part 3:** "People **should** take responsibility for their health."
