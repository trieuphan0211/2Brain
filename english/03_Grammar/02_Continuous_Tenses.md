# Continuous Tenses

## Rule

Continuous tenses describe actions happening now, around now, or at a specific moment in the past/future.

## Formulas

| Tense | Formula | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Present continuous | S + am/is/are + V-ing | I **am studying** now. |
| Past continuous | S + was/were + V-ing | She **was reading** when I called. |
| Present perfect continuous | S + have/has been + V-ing | They **have been waiting** for an hour. |

## Common Mistakes at Band 3.0

1. ❌ I **study** now. → ✅ I **am studying** now.
2. ❌ She **was** play football. → ✅ She **was playing** football.
3. ❌ They **have been wait** here. → ✅ They **have been waiting** here.
4. ❌ I **am** not **understanding**. → ✅ I **do not understand**. (some verbs are not usually continuous)

## Practice Sentences

1. Look! It __________ (rain). (present continuous)
2. At 8 p.m. yesterday, I __________ (watch) TV. (past continuous)
3. She __________ (work) here since 2020. (present perfect continuous)
4. They __________ (not / sleep) now. (present continuous)
5. We __________ (drive) when the accident happened. (past continuous)

## IELTS Context

- **Speaking Part 2:** "What are you doing these days?" → "I **am preparing** for IELTS."
- **Writing Task 1:** Use present continuous for trends happening now.
