# Future Tenses

## Rule

Use different future forms for predictions, plans, and spontaneous decisions.

## Formulas

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| will | prediction, promise, spontaneous decision | I **will** help you. |
| going to | plan, intention | I **am going to** study abroad. |
| present continuous for future | fixed arrangement | I **am meeting** her tomorrow. |

## Common Mistakes at Band 3.0

1. ❌ I **will** going to school. → ✅ I **am going to** school.
2. ❌ I **will go** to the party if I am free. → ✅ I **will go** to the party if I am free. (OK) / I **am going to** visit my friend. (plan)
3. ❌ She **will** has a baby. → ✅ She **is going to** have a baby.
4. ❌ I **go** to the doctor tomorrow. → ✅ I **am going** to the doctor tomorrow.

## Practice Sentences

1. I think it __________ (rain) tomorrow. (will)
2. She __________ (buy) a new phone next month. (going to)
3. We __________ (have) dinner with friends on Saturday. (present continuous)
4. __________ you __________ (help) me? (will)
5. I __________ (not / go) to work tomorrow. (going to)

## IELTS Context

- **Speaking Part 1/3:** "What will you do after the exam?" → "I **am going to** apply for a master's program."
- **Writing Task 2:** Use "will" for predictions about the future.
