# True / False / Not Given

## Rule

- **True:** The statement agrees with the information in the passage.
- **False:** The statement contradicts the information in the passage.
- **Not Given:** The information is not mentioned in the passage.

## Common Mistakes

1. Confusing **False** and **Not Given**.
2. Adding your own knowledge.
3. Matching words but not meaning.

## Strategy

1. Find the keywords in the statement.
2. Locate the matching part of the passage.
3. Read carefully — the answer is often a paraphrase.
4. If you cannot find the information, choose **Not Given**.

## Example

Passage: "The city has many parks."
- Statement: "The city has green spaces." → **True** (paraphrase)
- Statement: "The city has no parks." → **False**
- Statement: "The city has the best parks in the country." → **Not Given**
